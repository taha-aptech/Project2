2019-COVID-19 virus discovered in Wuhan

In December 2019, the first cases of a new respiratory illness were reported in Wuhan, China. The illness, caused by a novel coronavirus named SARS-CoV-2

  • First cases reported in Wuhan, China.
  • Initial investigations linked the outbreak to a seafood market.
  • China alerted the World Health Organization (WHO).
  • Early measures included isolating patients and closing the market.

2020-Global pandemic declared

In early 2020, COVID-19 spread globally, prompting the World Health Organization (WHO) to declare it a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on January 30, 2020, and a pandemic on March 11, 2020.

  • WHO declared a Public Health Emergency on January 30.
  • Pandemic status announced on March 11.
  • Countries implemented lockdowns and travel bans.
  • Healthcare systems were strengthened to handle patient surges.
  • Research and development of vaccines accelerated.
  • Vaccination campaigns began by the end of the year.

2021 - Vaccine Rollout & Variants

Vaccines became available to the general public in early 2021. However, new variants such as Delta and Alpha emerged, complicating the pandemic response.

  • Vaccine Distribution: January 2021 onwards
  • New Variants: Alpha, Beta, Delta
  • Global Vaccination Efforts: March - December 2021

2022 - Living with COVID

Many countries began transitioning to "living with COVID" by increasing vaccination rates and implementing less restrictive measures. However, surges in cases still occurred with new variants.

  • Vaccine Boosters: January - June 2022
  • Omicron Variant Surge: November - December 2022

2023 - A New Phase

By 2023, many countries started to phase out pandemic-era restrictions. There was a global push to return to normalcy, but COVID-19 remained a threat in various parts of the world.

  • End of Emergency: May 2023 (varied by region)
  • Continued Vaccination and Research: 2023

2024 - Ongoing Impact and Monitoring

COVID-19 remains an ongoing issue with surveillance efforts continuing globally. Variants continue to evolve, and healthcare systems are vigilant in monitoring trends.

  • Long COVID Awareness: 2024
  • Monitoring New Variants: Ongoing
