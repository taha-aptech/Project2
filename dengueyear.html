1950 - First Documented Epidemics

During the 1950s, large-scale epidemics of Dengue Fever were recorded in Southeast Asia, particularly in the Philippines and Thailand.

  • First severe outbreaks of dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF).
  • Virus transmission linked to urbanization and mosquito spread.

1970 - Dengue Spreads Worldwide

By the 1970s, dengue had expanded to multiple regions, including the Americas and Africa, due to increased global travel and urbanization.

  • Dengue becomes endemic in more than 9 countries.
  • Multiple dengue virus serotypes identified.

1990 - Rapid Increase in Dengue Cases

The 1990s saw a dramatic rise in dengue infections, with numerous outbreaks occurring in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

  • Dengue becomes a major public health concern.
  • Severe dengue cases (DHF) increase significantly.

2000 - Dengue Becomes Global Threat

By the 2000s, dengue had become a major global health challenge, with frequent outbreaks and rising mortality rates.

  • WHO declares dengue a major public health threat.
  • Research into vaccines and mosquito control intensifies.

2019 - Worst Dengue Outbreak in History

The year 2019 saw the highest number of dengue cases worldwide, affecting millions of people across Asia, South America, and Africa.

  • Over 4.2 million reported cases globally.
  • Record-breaking outbreaks in Southeast Asia and Latin America.

2024 - Dengue Prevention Efforts Continue

As of 2024, efforts to control dengue focus on vaccine distribution, mosquito eradication, and raising public awareness.

  • Wider use of dengue vaccines in endemic regions.
  • Implementation of advanced mosquito control strategies.
  • Research continues to develop better treatments.
