About Viruses

Understanding the world of viruses and their impact on health.

What are Viruses?

Viruses are microscopic infectious agents that can only replicate inside the living cells of an organism. They are composed of genetic material (DNA or RNA) surrounded by a protein coat and, in some cases, a lipid envelope.

How do Viruses Spread?

Viruses can spread through various means, including direct contact, airborne transmission, and contaminated surfaces. Understanding these transmission methods is crucial for prevention and control.

Impact on Health

Viruses can cause a range of diseases, from mild illnesses like the common cold to severe diseases such as HIV/AIDS and COVID-19. Vaccination and hygiene practices are essential in controlling viral infections.

Prevention and Control

Preventive measures include vaccination, hand hygiene, and avoiding close contact with infected individuals. Public health initiatives play a vital role in controlling outbreaks.
