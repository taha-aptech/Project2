1831 - The First Major Cholera Pandemic

The first major cholera pandemic began in India and spread to Russia and Europe, marking the start of a series of global cholera outbreaks.

  • First recognized as a global health threat in India.
  • Spread across the Middle East to Russia and Europe.
  • Large-scale fatalities in major cities.

1850 - Third Cholera Pandemic

The third cholera pandemic became the deadliest, particularly affecting Europe and Asia, causing widespread panic and awareness of the disease.

  • Notable for widespread deaths in London and other European cities.
  • Heightened awareness about sanitation and public health measures.
  • New investigations into choleraâ€™s transmission by contaminated water.

1900 - The Disease in the Americas

Cholera began spreading to the Americas, particularly affecting Cuba and the United States, after earlier outbreaks in Asia and Europe.

  • Cholera outbreaks in Cuba and South America.
  • International measures were introduced to prevent spread between continents.

1970 - The Seventh Cholera Pandemic

The seventh pandemic began in Indonesia and spread globally, marking the ongoing challenge of cholera in the modern world.

  • Ongoing spread to Africa, Asia, and Latin America.
  • Introduction of oral rehydration therapy (ORT) to treat cholera patients.

2000 - Cholera in Africa

Cholera became a significant public health issue in sub-Saharan Africa, with widespread outbreaks across several countries.

  • Massive cholera outbreaks in countries like Zimbabwe, Malawi, and Kenya.
  • Ongoing public health campaigns focused on safe water and sanitation.

2010 - Cholera Outbreak in Haiti

The cholera outbreak in Haiti became one of the deadliest in modern times, exacerbated by poor infrastructure after the 2010 earthquake.

  • Haiti's cholera outbreak led to thousands of deaths and widespread suffering.
  • International aid efforts focused on providing clean water and medical treatment.

2020 - Cholera Remains a Threat

While cholera has become less prevalent in certain areas, it remains a significant threat in countries with poor sanitation systems.

  • Cholera outbreaks in Yemen, Bangladesh, and Africa.
  • Ongoing vaccination campaigns and better access to clean water in some regions.
